# Dark matter as an *index*, not a substance — six ontological reframings (with discriminators)

**Thesis under test.** Stop treating "dark matter" as a substance with an observer-independent
value. Treat (part of) it as an **index**: the predictive residual a coarse, positions/velocity-
limited observer must invent to describe fine, velocity-resolved self-gravitating dynamics. The
ODD program's tools (predictive≠causal privilege, observer-indexed description, coarse-graining)
can *measure* that residual instead of asserting it.

**The disciplined boundary (stated up front, not buried).** This cannot be *all* of dark matter.
Milky-Way phase-space dynamics require simultaneous radial+vertical field consistency that favors
particle DM over modified gravity. So the honest, fundable claim is **not** "DM is an illusion" —
it is:

> **inferred DM = an observer-independent core + an observer-relative residual, and our unique
> contribution is to _measure the residual fraction_ rather than assert it is 0% or 100%.**

Each reframing below is anchored to (a) a real research lineage and (b) our own machinery, and
carries a **discriminator** so it does not stay metaphysics. Honesty labels: **[NUMBER NOW]** =
runnable in this rig and already producing a figure; **[NEEDS OP]** = idea is real but the
operational definition isn't nailed yet; **[INTERPRETIVE]** = a framing, not a separate measurement;
**[BRIDGE]** = high-risk/high-reward link to another community.

## 1. DM is predictive, not causal — the gravitational case of our signature dissociation  [INTERPRETIVE + teeth from the merger blind-window]
Our robust cross-system result: an observable can top the *predictive* ranking without being the
*causal* handle (velocity dispersion is ~85% "temperature" proxy; `f_peri` is the real handle).
**Reframe:** "dark matter" is the predictively-optimal effective field a coarse observer adds to
fit orbits — not a causal participant. **Teeth:** the merger blind-window shows inferred dynamical
*mass* lags the true state by ~+1.5 t_dyn in non-equilibrium phase space — a live demonstration
that some "missing mass" is an *estimator artifact of applying equilibrium predictors to non-
homologous systems*. **Discriminator:** the residual should move under intervention (mergers,
tidal shocks) on the phase-space relaxation timescale, not track baryons.

## 2. DM is misfiled velocity structure — a phase-space phenomenon read as a mass phenomenon  [NUMBER NOW ✓]
**Reframe:** "missing mass" is partly *missing information about the velocity distribution*,
projected onto configuration space as fictitious density. A velocity-aware observer needs less
dark matter than a positions/isotropy-blind one — for the *same halo*.
**Lineage / honesty:** this is the **mass–anisotropy degeneracy** (Binney; Wolf et al. 2010;
Walker & Peñarrubia) — *known physics*. What is new is not the degeneracy but the **ODD framing +
measurement**: define an *observer-relative DM residual fraction* and show it is the same velocity-
structure knob (β; and via #1 the orbit type) that drives the apparent core–cusp.
**Discriminator + RESULT (`dm_residual.py`):** equilibrium Osipkov-Merritt NFW haloes, identical
ρ(r)/M(<r), only β differs. A β-blind observer (isotropic spherical Jeans on the same ρ, σ_r)
over-infers enclosed mass by:

| halo | ⟨β⟩ (0.1–0.5) | invented DM frac (M_iso−M_true)/M_true |
|------|---------------|-----------------------------------------|
| isotropic (control) | −0.01 | **−9% ≈ 0** ✓ bug-guard |
| radial r_a=0.40 | 0.28 | **+37%** |
| radial r_a=0.20 | 0.55 | **+117%** |
| radial r_a=0.10 | 0.80 | **+184%** |

Jeans-check column (M_aware/M_true, using the *true* β) holds ~0.90 across the ladder → the
machinery is sound and the residual is a real β-blindness artifact, not a bug.
**Caveats (load-bearing):** (i) this is the *known* degeneracy, reframed — cite it, don't "discover"
it; (ii) the β-blind observer here is *generous* (given true 3-D σ_r; a real LOS observer's σ_los
projection carries additional β-dependence), so this isolates the Jeans-2β slice of the full
degeneracy; (iii) DM-only equilibrium sandbox — a proof-of-concept of the *residual measurement*,
not a claim about any specific galaxy. Figure: `outputs/figures/dm_residual.png`.

## 3. DM is scale-relative — a coarse-graining/RG residual, not a fixed field  [NUMBER NOW ✓ — but WEAK]
**Reframe:** "DM density" is the correction generated by describing fine-grained gravity at a
*chosen* resolution; different observers (different resolution scales) infer *different* DM — a "DM
RG flow." Lineage: backreaction/timescape (arXiv:1012.0784); coarse-grained thermodynamics with
observation-dependent quantities but observation-independent laws (PRResearch gcyn-18tv).
**Non-circular operationalization (`dm_scaleflow.py`):** the naive "smooth the density" version is
the trivial-zero trap — smoothing *conserves* mass, no flow. Instead sweep the OBSERVER's radial
resolution λ (the scale below which they can't resolve gradients) and run spherical Jeans on their
λ-resolved kinematics. The Jeans mass depends on d ln(ρσ_r²)/d ln r; a λ-observer measures that
gradient as a SECANT over step λ, biased wherever the profile CURVES. Same fixed equilibrium
halo (isotropic, β~0 → isolates scale from #2's anisotropy). **RESULT: real but WEAK.** As λ→r_s,
the coarse observer under-infers inner DM (secant flattens the inward-steepening NFW gradient):

| r | flow M_inf(λ)/M_inf(λ_min)−1 at λ=0.03 / 0.06 / 0.12 / 0.24 |
|---|---|
| 0.15 | 0% / −1% / −5% / — |
| 0.20 (=r_s) | 0% / −0% / −2% / **−10%** |
| 0.30 | 0% / −0% / −1% / −3% |
| 0.40 | 0% / −0% / −0% / −2% |

Monotonic in λ, concentrated at r ≲ r_s, sign physically correct — pre-registered prediction
confirmed. **Double-check note:** a raw-binned estimator gave a sign-bouncing ~7% that was
noise-dominated; smoothing ln P(ln r) to isolate genuine curvature turned it into this clean ~10%
monotonic signal (real vs noise, resolved). **Honest magnitude:** ~10% peak vs #2's 37–184% → the
DM residual is **dominantly velocity-access (#2), with a ~4–18× weaker spatial-resolution
component (#3).** Same honesty label as #2: reframes the known finite-resolution Jeans bias as
scale-indexed DM; novelty is the flow curve + ODD packaging. Figure: `outputs/figures/dm_scaleflow.png`.

## 4. DM is gravitating information — entropy of the unresolved degrees of freedom  [BRIDGE]
Lineage: Verlinde emergent/entropic gravity (arXiv:1611.02269); Emergent Dark Matter
(arXiv:2511.09034); Quantum Memory Matrix. **Reframe:** gravity sources partly from the *entropy
gap* between an observer's coarse description and the fine truth → DM amount is observer-indexed =
unresolved-information content. **Discriminator (novel, runnable-in-principle):** DM excess should
correlate with *phase-space entropy / mixing complexity*, not baryon count — both computable in
sims. Highest risk, highest reward; separates "info-field DM" from "particle DM."

## 5. Observer-relative dark matter — no fact of the matter about its *value*, only its residual  [INTERPRETIVE — the manifesto]
The apex framing fusing 1–4: DM is not a substance with an observer-independent density; it is an
index on (observer, task, scale). **Boundary (see top):** = observer-independent core + observer-
relative residual; the contribution is to *measure the residual fraction*. #2 is the first brick.

## 6. DM is a thermodynamic state, not a mass inventory  [INTERPRETIVE — mechanism-level]
Our SIDM/gravothermal work lives in the negative-heat-capacity regime. **Reframe:** halo anomalies
(cores, cusps, diversity) are *thermodynamic states* of a coarse-grained gravitating fluid —
entropy-driven, history-dependent — not readouts of particle abundance. The
gravothermal-collapse-robust-to-feedback result hints the *state*, not the *substance*, is invariant.
